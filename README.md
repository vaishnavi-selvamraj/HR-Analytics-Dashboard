# 📊 HR Analytics Dashboard
 
An interactive Power BI dashboard providing end-to-end visibility into employee attrition, demographics, and workforce composition.
 
![image_alt}(https://github.com/vaishnavi-selvamraj/HR-Analytics-Dashboard/blob/main/Screenshot%20(70).png?raw=true)
 
## 🔍 Overview
 
This dashboard helps HR teams and business leaders understand attrition patterns, identify risk areas, and make data-driven workforce decisions. Built in **Power BI Desktop**, it features dynamic filtering by age group and cross-visual interactivity.

## 🧩 Dashboard Features
 
### Filters
- **Age Group slicer** — Filter all visuals by: 18–25, 26–35, 36–45, 46–55, 55+
### Visuals Included
 
| Visual | Description |
|---|---|
| KPI Cards | Total employees, active employees, attrition count, rate, avg. age & experience |
| Attrition by Department | Donut chart showing attrition share across departments |
| Attrition by Salary Slab | Horizontal bar chart (No vs Yes) across salary bands |
| Attrition by Job Role & Job Satisfaction | Matrix showing satisfaction scores 1–4 per role |
| Age Group Distribution | Bar chart of headcount across age bands |
| Attrition by Gender | Pie chart — Male vs Female split |
| Attrition Trend by Experience | Line chart showing attrition over years of experience |
| Department-Wise Employee Count | Horizontal bar chart ranking all departments by headcount |

## 🚨 Key Insights
 
- **Highest attrition** is in the **6–10 LPA salary slab** (399 employees, with 73 attritions)
- **Sales** department drives the largest attrition share (~23%)
- **Laboratory Technicians** show the highest attrition count among all job roles (59 total)
- Attrition **spikes early in tenure** — employees with 0–2 years of experience leave the most
- **Male employees** account for ~63% of the workforce
